Fuzzy logic denotes the logic of uncertainty as it pertains to real world probabilities.

## + Formalism +
The biggest feature about fuzzy logic is that instead of having distribution rules for conjunction, disjunction, and negation, those operations are represented by numerical functions
- $x \land y = \min\{ x, y \}$
- $x \lor y = \max\{ x, y \}$
- $\neg x = 1 - x$

## + Quantum Logic +
Given that fuzzy logic deals with *degrees* of truth, it could be said that there is a very good one-to-one equivalency between the two. 
### ++ Squaring ++
The one major point of difference between fuzzy logic and quantum logic is the lack of a "squaring" operator. In [[Quantum Mechanics|quantum mechanics]], a key notation of the probability distribution of a wavefunction is the possibility for negative probability.
