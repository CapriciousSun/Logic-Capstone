Operators are how information is extracted from [[Wavefunction|wavefunctions]].
## + Formalism +
Operators represent some *eigenvalue* of the wavefunction, with each operation represented as
$$\hat{A}\ket{\psi} = a\ket{\psi} $$
where $a$ is some eigenvalue of $\psi$. 
### ++ Simultaneity ++
Simultaneous eigenvalues obey a commutation relation where
$$[\hat{A}, \hat{B}] = 0$$
meaning that they can be observed at the same time with no uncertainty. It would be reasonable, then, to say that standard distributivity laws would apply. For example, the Hamiltonian $\hat{H}$ and momentum $\hat{p}$ are simultaneous. However, there are operators that are not simultaneous, so therefore not distributive. An example of non-simultaneous operators are position $\hat{x}$ and momentum $\hat{p}$.
