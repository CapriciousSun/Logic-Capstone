Quantum mechanics is used to describe the interactions that happen at the atomic level. 

## Propositions
Quantum mechanics is an entirely probabilistic field, meaning that all propositions are entirely [[Boolean|non-Boolean]]. Instead, propositions are of the form "a physical quantity $A$ lies in the range $B$", which is represented by some projection onto a Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$. Due to the non-Boolean nature of quantum mechanical propositions, a non-distributive-orthocomplemented lattice is used for its representation, where states correspond to measures on said lattice.
